Dr. S jaishankar

INTRODUCTION

Subrahmanyam Jaishankar, widely known as S. Jaishankar, is an Indian diplomat and politician who served as the Minister of External Affairs of India from 2019 to 2023.

With his extensive diplomatic experience and strategic vision, Jaishankar played a crucial role in shaping India's foreign policy and representing the country on the global stage.

Early Life and Education

S. Jaishankar was born on January 9, 1955, in New Delhi, India. He comes from a distinguished family with a strong background in politics and diplomacy. His father, K. Subrahmanyam, was a renowned strategic affairs analyst, and his mother, Sulochana, was an author and classical dancer. Jaishankar completed his schooling in Delhi and then pursued a Bachelor's degree in Economics from St. Stephen's College, University of Delhi. He went on to earn a Master's degree in Political Science and an M.Phil . and Ph.D. in International Relations from Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU) in Delhi.

Diplomatic Career

S. Jaishankar joined the Indian Foreign Service in 1977 and served in various key positions throughout his career. He held diplomatic assignments in countries like Russia, Japan, Singapore, and the United States. His expertise in strategic affairs and his ability to handle complex negotiations earned him a stellar reputation among his peers.

Minister of External Affairs

In May 2019, S. Jaishankar was appointed as the Minister of External Affairs of India under the leadership of Prime Minister Narendra Modi. As the chief architect of India's foreign policy, Jaishankar focused on promoting India's interests, safeguarding national security, and projecting India as a responsible global power. During his tenure, Jaishankar successfully navigated complex international relations, including trade disputes, regional conflicts, and global challenges such as the COVID-19 pandemic. He actively engaged with countries around the world, fostering stronger diplomatic ties and building strategic partnerships.

Achievements and Contributions

. Jaishankar's contributions to Indian diplomacy and foreign policy are numerous. Some of his notable achievements include:

  • Strengthening India's strategic partnerships with major powers, including the United States, Russia, and Japan.
  • Playing a key role in resolving the Doklam border standoff between India and China.
  • India's diplomatic efforts to counter terrorism and promote regional security.
  • Leading India's successful campaign for a non-permanent seat on the United Nations Security Council for the term 2021-2022.
  • Pioneering the "Vaccine Maitri" initiative, which provided COVID-19 vaccines to countries around the world.
